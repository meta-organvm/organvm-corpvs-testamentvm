## Critical Success Factors

### 1. Documentation-First Approach

Don't code first, document second. In Sprints 1-2, writing comprehensive READMEs is your primary task. This determines everything downstream.

### 2. Phase Isolation

Don't mix phases. Complete Phase 1 (documentation) before Phase 2 (validation). Complete Phase 2 before Phase 3 (integration). Partial phases are messy.

### 3. All 8 Organs Represented at Launch

All 8 organs must be represented at launch with at least one flagship repo fully documented. Remaining repos may launch as stubs or in-progress (compatible with Bronze/Silver/Gold tiered approach). This ensures the meta-system is visible as a coherent whole from day 1.

### 4. ORGAN-V as Narrative

The essays in ORGAN-V aren't optional. They're where you tell the story of what this system is and why it matters. Without them, external evaluators see 44 repos, not a coherent system.

### 5. Make Decisions Visible

Every decision about governance, promotion criteria, dependency rules should be explicit in the documentation. This is what differentiates your work from typical open-source projects.
