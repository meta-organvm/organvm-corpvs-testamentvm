## Application Method

**Web form** at https://nlnet.nl/propose/ — select "NGI Zero Commons Fund"

## Form Fields

### Section 1: Contact
- Name, email, phone, organisation, country

### Section 2: Call
- Select "NGI Zero Commons Fund"

### Section 3: Project
- **Proposal name**
- **Website/wiki** (optional)
- **Abstract** — full project + expected outcomes (200-500 words)
- **Background** — prior relevant work (100-300 words)

### Section 4: Support
- **Budget** — EUR 5K-50K (first-time applicants max 50K)
- **Budget justification** — task-level breakdown with explicit rates (CRITICAL — 30% of score)
- **Other funding sources** — past and present
- **Comparison** — against existing efforts (show you know the landscape)
- **Technical challenges** — honest risk assessment
- **Ecosystem** — community engagement, standards, deployment plan

### Section 5: AI Disclosure (mandatory)
- Whether AI was used, model, prompts, unedited output

## Evaluation Criteria

Must exceed 5.0/7.0 in first-stage scoring:

| Criterion | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Relevance / impact / strategic potential | **40%** |
| Technical excellence / feasibility | 30% |
| Cost effectiveness / value for money | 30% |

Then minimum 2 expert reviewers in second stage.

## Critical Requirements

- All software must be **open source** (recognized license)
- R&D must be primary focus (not deployment of existing solutions)
- **European Dimension** — EU/Associated Country applicants preferred. Non-EU need "exceptional quality" and "unique technical expertise"
- WCAG compliance for delivered software
- Open standards where possible
- Projects >50K EUR may require independent security audit

## What Makes a Strong Application

- Frame as **internet commons infrastructure**, not internal tooling
- Show who benefits and how many people
- Genuine R&D component (not just integration)
- Task-level budget with rates (not lump sum)
- Sustainability plan beyond the grant
- Know the landscape (comparison field)
- Honest technical risk assessment

## Timeline

~6 months from application to acceptance (based on funded project experience).
Milestone-based payments via MoU.

## Relevant Funded Projects

TrailBase (BaaS), F-Droid reproducible builds, GoActivityPub (federation tooling), C/C++ Package Registry, allowd (policy rules), p2panda (collaboration infra), Typed Nix (infra-as-code).

## ORGANVM Framing for NLnet

**Position as:** Open-source governance infrastructure for the digital commons — promotion state machines, dependency validation, and registry management that any large open-source ecosystem can adopt.

**R&D component:** The governance engine's formal verification of dependency flows, the promotion state machine as a generalizable pattern, the seed.yaml contract system as a novel approach to ecosystem coordination.

**Impact:** Benefits any multi-repo open-source project struggling with governance at scale.
