# Creative Capital Award 2027 — Application Research

**Date:** 2026-03-24
**Deadline:** April 2, 2026, 3:00 PM Eastern (9 DAYS)
**Status:** Research complete — ready to draft

---

## Key Facts

- **Amount:** $15,000–$50,000 unrestricted, 5-year engagement
- **~50 artists funded** per cycle
- **State of Art Prize:** $10,000 per state (automatic consideration)
- **Method:** Web form (not PDF)
- **Source:** [2027 Handbook PDF](https://creative-capital.org/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/2027-Creative-Capital-Application-Handbook.pdf)

## Eligibility

- US citizen/permanent resident/O-1 visa
- 25+ years old
- 5+ years professional artistic practice
- NOT in degree program
- Never previously received Creative Capital Award

## CRITICAL: Technology Is a Sub-Discipline

Technology is NOT a standalone primary category. Must apply under **Visual Arts** or **Performing Arts** as primary, then select **Technology** as one of 3 sub-disciplines.

Technology includes: games, digital media, AI, robotics, AR/VR, internet art, bio art, etc.

## Round I Fields (All You Submit April 2)

### Profile
- Short bio: **50 words**
- Long bio: **200 words** (artistic career focus)
- Resume: PDF upload (5+ years, art only, ~1 page)

### Project
- Title: **10 words max**
- One-line description: **50 words / 400 characters**
- Project description: **500 words / 3,550 characters**
- Estimated completion date

### Four Questions (150 words / 1,050 characters EACH)

**Q1 — Innovation:** "How does your project take an original and imaginative approach to content and form?"

**Q2 — Context & Influences:** "Place your work in context. What are the main influences upon your work?"

**Q3 — Impact:** "What impact do you hope? What audiences/communities will it engage?"

**Q4 — Catalytic Moment:** "How is this a catalytic moment? How will this project catalyze your growth?"

### Work Sample (ONE, from completed past work)
- Image: up to 5 images of single work, PDF/JPG/GIF/PNG, 72+ dpi, 5MB max
- Video: max 5 minutes, YouTube/Vimeo link
- Caption: title, materials, date, description (**100 words / 750 characters**)

## NO budget or timeline in Round I. Those are Round II (by invitation, June 2026).

## Selection Criteria (weighted order)

1. **Artistic Strength** (HIGHEST): Compelling, original, imaginative, innovative, impactful?
2. **Feasibility & Capacity**: Resources understood? Professional landscape awareness?
3. **Timing**: Catalytic career moment?

## Strategic Notes

- Round I is ~1,000 words total across 4 questions + 500-word description + 1 work sample. Every word counts.
- Character limits include spaces — write to character count.
- "Does this project need to happen now?" and "Is this artist the best equipped?" are the implicit questions.
- They want EARLY stage projects. Not finishing funds.
- Be concrete. Avoid jargon. Communicate urgency and originality.
- Work sample: drop viewers into the action. No slow buildup.

## ORGANVM Framing for Creative Capital

**Primary discipline:** Visual Arts
**Sub-disciplines:** Technology, Social Practice, Installation (or Sound Art if Organ II work)

**Frame as:** A living creative system — 117 interconnected repositories forming a self-governing artwork that merges generative art, AI orchestration, and institutional governance. The governance IS the art. The system IS the creative practice.

**Innovation angle:** No artist has built a self-governing institutional system as their primary creative medium. The promotion state machine, dependency flows, and organ architecture are aesthetic choices as much as engineering ones.

**Catalytic moment:** Solo practitioner who has built the system to critical mass — now needs Creative Capital to transform it from private infrastructure into public creative practice.
